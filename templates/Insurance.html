Insurance Information

At Your Healthcare, we understand the importance of having comprehensive healthcare coverage. Here is some information about insurance options:

  • Health Maintenance Organization (HMO)

  • Coverage: Requires members to choose a primary care physician (PCP) and get referrals to see specialists.
    Generally, only covers care provided by in-network doctors and facilities.

  • Costs: Lower out-of-pocket costs but limited flexibility in choosing healthcare providers.

  • Preferred Provider Organization (PPO):

  • Coverage: Offers more flexibility in choosing healthcare providers, both in-network and out-
    of-network. Referrals are not required to see specialists.

  • Costs: Higher premiums and more flexibility, but lower costs when using in-network providers.

  • Exclusive Provider Organization (EPO):

  • Coverage: Similar to PPO but typically doesn't cover any out-of-network care except in emergencies.

  • Costs: Moderate premiums, and lower out-of-pocket costs when using in-network providers.

  • High Deductible Health Plan (HDHP) with Health Savings Account (HSA):

  • Coverage: Requires higher out-of-pocket spending before coverage kicks in. Often paired
    with an HSA to save money for medical expenses tax-free.

  • Costs: Lower premiums but higher out-of-pocket costs until the deductible is met.

  • Catastrophic Health Insurance:

  • Coverage: Provides coverage for major medical expenses after a high deductible is met.
    Typically for young, healthy individuals.

  • Costs: Lowest premiums but high out-of-pocket costs until the deductible is reached.
